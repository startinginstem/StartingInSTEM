1857

Elizabeth Blackwell established the New York Infirmary for Women and Children while also being an advocate for women pursing careers in medical fields.

1905

Nettie Stevens is credited with discovering the difference between X and Y chromosomes leading to further developments in genetics

1911

Marie Curie discovered radium and polonium. She won a Nobel Peace Prize for this discovery.

1916

Margaret Sanger was an activist for birth control and established the first birth control center that led to Planned Parenthood

1928

Amelia Earhart was the first woman to fly across the Atlantic ocean and is seen as a pioneer for women pursuing typically male-dominate jobs

1934

Florence Seibert discovered the active agent in tuberculin which could be purified and later became the standardized test for Tuberculosis.

1952

Grace Hopper is credited with creating the first compiler for computer programs. After her MANY accomplishments in both the military and in science, she became a teacher to pass on her knowledge.

1983

Sally Ride interviewed in a very competitive pool of applicants and joined a NASA space team. In 1983, she became the first female astronaut to travel to space

1988

V.R. Lalithambika is an Indian engineer who has worked on the technologies related to space travel for India. She is leading the team that plans to send astronauts into space in just 3 years.

2001

Narry Kim is a biochemist from South Korea who has been a pioneer in the study of microRNA and has helped develop technologies related to it.

2008

Funke Opeke is a Nigerian electrical engineer who founded Main Street Technologies and is working on improving connectivity across Nigeria.

2017

Alba Colon is a member of Hendrick Motorsports where she is an engineer working on racecars and helping to implement new technologies for future races.

2018

Ethel Cofie is the founder of Women in Tech Africa. She is working to pioneer the way for more women to become involved in STEM across Africa using her new program.
